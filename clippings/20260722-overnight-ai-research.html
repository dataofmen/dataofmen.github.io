  • Domain: execution, microstructure

  • Actionability: study

  • Why it matters: Prediction markets are becoming tradable signal venues, but their binary settlement makes inventory risk different from normal equities or futures. Henry should not read prediction-market price as a clean probability unless liquidity provision, inventory skew, and time-to-resolution risk are understood.   • Raw excerpt / abstract: “Due to the binary settlement structure in prediction markets, optimal market making leads to an optimization problem that is fundamentally different from the ones studied in classical settings… A market maker selects bid and ask quotes to maximize expected terminal wealth while controlling both mark-to-market inventory risk and the settlement risk of remaining positions at resolution.”
